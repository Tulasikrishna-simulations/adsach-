Abstract

What this paper is about

Digital commerce operates across two distinct layers of trust — whether a product is what the vendor claims, and whether the advertisement used to sell it psychologically suppresses the consumer's ability to ask that question.

We introduce a unified two-layer verification architecture bridging the Algorithmic Trust Engine (ATE) — a supply-chain-grounded product verification system — with the NTI-AD AI PAVE-C v2.0 framework — a real-time advertising manipulation detection system. The integration produces two novel scalar measures: the Unified Trust Index (UTI) and the Advertising Integrity Deficit (AID).


We extend the framework with five additional metrics — Linguistic Pressure Score (LPS), Emotional Density Vector (EDV), Semantic Exaggeration Coefficient (SEC), Psychological Entropy Score (H_ψ), and an enhanced Manipulation Pressure Index (MPI) — each grounded in dual-process cognitive theory.


We apply the unified framework to six documented cases from the Indian advertising landscape (Vimal Pan Masala, Maggi, Bournvita, Horlicks, KFC India, Fair & Lovely), all supported by ASCI rulings, FSSAI orders, NCPCR notices, and court judgments. We identify Quadrant II — genuine products sold through psychologically manipulative advertising — as the dominant configuration in Indian FMCG advertising, and the configuration most invisible to existing regulatory frameworks.

Contributions

What this paper contributes

01
2D Commercial Trust Space
A formal two-dimensional space with product truth (ATE score) on one axis and advertising transparency (NTI-AD) on the other. Every commercial interaction can be located and classified into four behaviorally distinct quadrants.
02
Unified Trust Index (UTI)
UTI = ATE × (NTI/100). Multiplicative integration correctly penalizes vendors who combine genuine products with manipulative advertising. A linear average would give dangerously misleading results.
03
Advertising Integrity Deficit (AID)
AID = ATE − UTI. The first formal definition of the gap between product quality and advertising honesty as a measurable scalar. High AID = genuine product sold through aggressive psychological manipulation.
04
PAVE-C v2.0 — Five New Metrics
LPS (linguistic force), EDV (7-dimensional emotional vector), SEC (exaggeration vs factual content ratio), H_ψ (Shannon entropy over consumer decision space), MPI (enhanced manipulation index). Each grounded in cognitive science literature.
05
Indian Advertising Case Corpus
Six documented cases grounded in ASCI rulings, FSSAI orders, NCPCR notices, and court judgments. The first application of a unified product-advertising transparency framework to the Indian consumer market.
06
ODE-Based Trust Growth Model
dT/dS = α(T_max − T). The Eden Condition: a dishonest vendor cannot buy trust through scan volume. Trust growth is gated by the coupling constant α = e^(−λΔ), which collapses to zero as mismatch Δ increases.
Mathematical Framework

The equations at the core

Every metric is formally defined. Every formula is implemented in running code.

ATE — Mismatch Norm
Δ = ‖V − C‖_F = √(Σᵢ (vᵢ − cᵢ)²)
V = physical reality vector from supply chain. C = advertised claim vector. Δ = 0 is perfect honesty.
ATE — Trust Growth ODE
dT/dS = α(T_max − T)
T(S) = T_max·(1 − e^(−αS))
α = e^(−λΔ)
The Eden Condition. Dishonest vendors (Δ→∞, α→0) cannot grow trust regardless of scan volume.
H_ψ — Psychological Entropy
H_ψ = −Σₖ P'(dₖ|ad)·log₂P'(dₖ|ad)
H_ψ_score = 1 − H_ψ/log₂(K)
Shannon entropy over consumer decision space (K=6). Manipulation systematically compresses decision freedom toward single action.
PAVE-C v2.0 — Final Score
DRS = 0.28·MPI + 0.22·(1−VI)
+ 0.18·(1−AS) + 0.18·H_ψ + 0.14·SEC
TS = (1−DRS)×(1−CBF)×100
Multiplicative structure: cognitive bypass amplifies the effect of deception. Weights sum to 1.00.
Unified Trust Index
UTI = ATE × (TS_NTI / 100)
AID = ATE × (1 − TS_NTI/100)
Multiplicative integration. ATE=95, NTI=10 → UTI=9.5. Linear average would give 52.5 — dangerously misleading.
EDV — Emotional Density Vector
EDV = [f_fear, f_desire, f_guilt,
f_excite, f_loss, f_pride, f_envy]ᵀ
EMI = |EDV|·(1 − valence_auth)
7-dimensional vector replaces scalar EAI. Fear-based and desire-based manipulation require different consumer interventions.

Celebrity endorsement does not constitute product recommendation — it is paid performance. This distinction is systematically exploited in Indian advertising targeting children and teenagers.
